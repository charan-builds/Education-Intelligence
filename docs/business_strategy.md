# Business Strategy

## Positioning

The product is no longer just an LMS-style learning app. The strongest positioning is:

> an AI-native learning and career platform for institutions, cohort programs, and ambitious learners that turns progress into job readiness

That positioning is defensible because the platform already has:

- adaptive roadmaps
- mentor AI with memory
- real-time collaboration
- career readiness and resume generation
- ecosystem/marketplace foundations
- ML and knowledge-graph layers
- autonomous guidance

## Customer Segments

### 1. Individual learners

Primary value:

- personalized learning plan
- AI mentor
- career readiness scoring
- interview preparation

Best motion:

- self-serve acquisition
- freemium conversion

### 2. Cohort operators and bootcamps

Primary value:

- student progress visibility
- mentor workflows
- intervention tooling
- job-readiness reporting

Best motion:

- inside sales
- pilot-to-paid conversion

### 3. Schools, universities, and enterprise learning teams

Primary value:

- tenant-level analytics
- role-based operations
- integrations
- governance and marketplace supply

Best motion:

- enterprise sales
- annual contracts

## Pricing Strategy

### Free

Who it is for:

- individual learners trying the product

Included:

- limited roadmap generation
- basic diagnostic
- limited AI mentor messages per month
- basic knowledge graph and progress tracking
- limited career readiness view

Purpose:

- acquisition
- habit formation
- showcase personalization before paywall

### Pro

Target price:

- `$19 to $39 / user / month`

Included:

- unlimited roadmap refreshes
- full AI mentor with memory
- advanced explain/explore/question generation
- career engine, resume builder, interview prep
- autonomous learning agent guidance
- advanced analytics and retention insights

Purpose:

- monetize serious learners
- maximize conversion from high-intent users

### Team / Cohort

Target price:

- `$149 to $499 / month` base
- plus per-seat pricing

Included:

- teacher and mentor dashboards
- cohort analytics
- intervention workflows
- collaboration features
- shared knowledge graph and progress intelligence

Purpose:

- monetize bootcamps, academies, training operators

### Enterprise

Target price:

- annual contract
- setup + platform fee
- optional usage-based AI surcharge

Included:

- SSO / advanced IAM
- API access
- white-label / branding controls
- marketplace and plugin ecosystem access
- custom analytics
- premium support and SLAs

Purpose:

- high-ACV revenue
- lower churn
- strategic logo acquisition

### Usage-Based Add-Ons

Use for:

- heavy AI usage
- premium interview simulation
- marketplace transactions
- API access beyond plan limits

Recommended metered units:

- AI mentor premium requests
- mock interview sessions
- API calls
- marketplace GMV take rate

## Revenue Model

## Growth Strategy

## Acquisition

Top channels:

- SEO around career and learning intent
- demo-led social content
- campus ambassadors and creator-teachers
- partnerships with bootcamps and cohort schools
- product-led sharing of resume/job-readiness artifacts

Best acquisition hooks:

- “Get your job-readiness score”
- “See the fastest path to become interview-ready”
- “Turn your weak topics into a concrete study plan”

## Activation

The first-session goal is:

- diagnostic completed
- roadmap generated
- first AI mentor interaction
- first career readiness output seen

This is the activation sequence because it shows personalization immediately.

## Retention Loops

Core loops:

- study -> improved readiness -> return to improve score further
- weak-topic detection -> mentor guidance -> roadmap update -> measurable progress
- due review -> retention improvement -> visible mastery gains
- resume/interview prep -> career progress -> repeat practice

Product mechanisms already aligned:

- notifications
- streaks and XP
- mentor memory
- autonomous agent nudges
- retention review scheduling

## Referral Loops

Natural referral assets:

- job-readiness report
- resume preview
- interview-prep scorecard
- public learning progress snapshots for cohorts

Recommended referral mechanics:

- invite a friend for extra AI credits
- teacher referral rewards for cohort onboarding
- marketplace creator rewards for course/resource uploads

- `weekly active learners who complete at least one guided learning action`

This is better than raw DAU because it aligns with learning value and retention.

## Product Roadmap

### Short Term: 0-3 months

- tighten onboarding around diagnostic -> roadmap -> mentor -> career score
- launch clear plan packaging in the UI
- ship billing instrumentation and usage tracking
- expose key business metrics in admin analytics
- improve invitation and referral flows
- polish enterprise-ready security and audit controls

### Mid Term: 3-9 months

- launch creator marketplace supply
- expand team/cohort workflows
- add experiments and pricing tests
- improve career engine with employer-facing outcomes
- add public API onboarding for partners
- introduce enterprise onboarding and SSO

### Long Term: 9-18 months

- multi-region enterprise deployment
- full ecosystem monetization
- partner distribution channels
- benchmarked job outcome analytics
- deeper AI autonomy and coach workflows
- internationalization and localized market expansion

## GTM Sequence

Recommended order:

1. win individual users with the career story
2. use proof from engaged learners to sell cohorts and bootcamps
3. use cohort analytics and governance to close enterprise
4. layer marketplace and APIs once demand-side retention is strong

## Immediate Execution Priorities

If the goal is to become business-ready quickly, the highest-leverage next moves are:

1. formalize packaging and entitlements in-product
2. instrument activation, conversion, and retention metrics end-to-end
3. make job-readiness and AI mentor the core acquisition narrative
4. add billing and usage enforcement
5. run pilot cohorts with measurable before/after learner outcomes
